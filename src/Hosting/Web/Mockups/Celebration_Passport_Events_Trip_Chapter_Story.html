The First Year

A chapter of Kumar & Riya's Passport · 6 moments · Jan – Dec 2026

It began, as these things often do, in the middle of moving boxes. The first home was smaller than either of you expected and louder than you'd hoped — but by the second week, it was simply home.

Spring brought Udaipur: a weekend meant for two, spent mostly getting lost on purpose. The photos from that trip still make you laugh — the wrong turn near Fateh Sagar that became the whole story of the weekend, the rooftop dinner you almost missed, the palace lit gold against a sky that looked staged but wasn't.

"We got lost finding the restaurant, and it became our favorite story."

Not every page of this year was a postcard. There was a Tuesday in January neither of you handled well, and a quiet apology that mattered more than the fight ever did. It's in here too — because a year is not just its best weekend, it's everything that made the weekend feel earned.

By December, the boxes were long gone, replaced by a shelf of small, ordinary Sundays — the kind that don't photograph well but are the ones you'll miss the most, someday, without knowing exactly why.
